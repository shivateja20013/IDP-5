Kurnool

Kurnool is a city in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh and serves as the administrative headquarters of the Kurnool district. It has a rich history, cultural heritage, and several notable attractions.

City Information

Kurnool is situated in the western part of Andhra Pradesh, and it was the capital of Andhra State during the pre-independence era.
The city is known for its historical significance and as a center for trade and agriculture.

Tourist Spots

Konda Reddy Fort

Konda Reddy Fort, also known as Kurnool Fort, is a historical fortress with a rich history.
The fort offers panoramic views of the city and the surrounding landscape.

Mahanandi Temple

Mahanandi is a famous temple town located near Kurnool, known for the Mahanandiswara Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.
The temple's architecture and the natural spring that flows through the complex make it a peaceful pilgrimage site.

Mantralayam

Mantralayam is a major pilgrimage town located at the border of Andhra Pradesh and Karnataka.
It is famous for the Raghavendra Swamy Matha, a significant religious institution dedicated to Guru Raghavendra.

Oravakallu Rock Garden

Oravakallu Rock Garden is an interesting park with unique rock formations, sculptures, and a serene environment.
It's a great place for picnics and spending time in nature.

Nallamala Forests

The Nallamala Forests, located nearby, offer a range of outdoor activities like trekking and nature exploration.
The forests are rich in biodiversity and have several beautiful spots to explore.

Kurnool and its surrounding areas are a mix of historical, cultural, and natural attractions. The city's historical significance and proximity to other interesting places make it a good choice for tourists looking to explore this part of Andhra Pradesh.
