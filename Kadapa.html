Kadapa

Kadapa, also known as Cuddapah, is a city in the YSR Kadapa district of Andhra Pradesh, and it has several interesting places to visit.

City Information

Kadapa, also known as Cuddapah, is a city in the YSR Kadapa district of Andhra Pradesh, and it has several interesting places to visit.
The city is home to various cultural and architectural landmarks.

Tourist Spots

Gandikota Fort

Gandikota Fort, often referred to as the "Grand Canyon of India," is a major tourist attraction near Kadapa.
It is known for its breathtaking views of the Penna River and its dramatic landscape of deep gorges and red sandstone formations.

Belum Caves

Belum Caves, located in the Belum Village of Kurnool district, are a short drive from Kadapa.
These caves are among the largest and longest cave systems in India and are famous for their unique stalactite and stalagmite formations.

Sri Venkateswara Wildlife Sanctuary

The Sri Venkateswara Wildlife Sanctuary is located in the Kadapa and Chittoor districts
It's a protected area that houses a variety of wildlife, including leopards, Indian giant squirrels, and a wide range of bird species.

Brahmamgari Matham

This is a significant religious center dedicated to the sage Brahmananda Swami.
Pilgrims visit the Matham to pay their respects and seek blessings.

Pushpagiri Temples

The Pushpagiri Temples, also known as the Pushpagiri Peetam, is a group of ancient temples dedicated to Shiva.
These temples have a historical and religious significance and attract devotees.

Kadapa and its surrounding areas offer a mix of natural beauty, historical significance, and religious sites. The Gandikota Fort and Belum Caves, in particular, are unique attractions that draw visitors from near and far
